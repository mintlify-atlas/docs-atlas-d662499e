Andesite Mines are gray-toned caves where andesite stone is naturally concentrated, providing easy access to this building material.

## Overview

**Biome ID:** `ANDESITE_MINES`  
**Vanilla Replacement:** `minecraft:dripstone_caves`  
**Color Code:** `#A6A898`

## Terrain Palette

Andesite Mines feature a simple two-material palette:

### Surface Layers (2 blocks deep)
- Stone (dominant - 3 parts)
- Andesite (1 part)

### Subsurface Layer (1 block deep)
- Stone (dominant - 3 parts)
- Andesite (1 part)

### Deepslate Transition
Below Y-level 7, terrain uses the `ANDESITE_MINES_DEEPSLATE` palette.

<Note>
Andesite appears at a 1:3 ratio with stone, creating veins and pockets throughout the cave system.
</Note>

## Features

Andesite Mines are primarily defined by their terrain palette and do not include additional feature decorations.

## Environment

Andesite Mines provide a mining-focused cave biome with abundant gray andesite throughout. The gray tones create a neutral, industrial feel to these caves. Like other mine biomes, these feel like natural quarries optimized for resource gathering.

<Note>
This biome is excellent for gathering andesite for builds, particularly for players who prefer gray stone variants.
</Note>

## Palette Configuration

The terrain generation uses:
- **Sampler Type:** Cellular with domain warp
- **Frequency:** 0.08 (surface), 0.8 (subsurface)
- **Warp Frequency:** 0.01 (surface), 0.1 (subsurface)
- **Dimensions:** 2D sampling
