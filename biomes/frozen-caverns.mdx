The Frozen Caverns are frigid underground areas featuring ice, snow, and spectacular icicle formations hanging from ceilings and rising from floors.

## Overview

**Biome ID:** `FROZEN_CAVERNS`  
**Vanilla Replacement:** `minecraft:dripstone_caves`  
**Color Code:** `#91C9C4`

## Terrain Palette

The Frozen Caverns feature an icy, crystalline palette:

### Surface Layers (2 blocks deep)
- Stone
- Snow Block (dominant)
- Packed Ice
- Blue Ice

### Subsurface Layer (1 block deep)
- Stone
- Packed Ice
- Blue Ice
- Snow Block

### Deepslate Transition
Below Y-level 7, terrain transitions to the `FROZEN_CAVERNS_DEEPSLATE` palette.

## Features

### Preprocessors
- **Ceiling Icicles** (Substratum variant) - Large icicles hanging from cave ceilings
- **Ground Icicles** (Substratum variant) - Icicles rising from cave floors

### Slabs
- **Snow** - Snow layers cover floors where possible

### Landforms
- **Giant Ceiling Icicles** (Substratum variant) - Massive ice formations hanging from above

## Environment

Frozen Caverns create a cold, pristine underground environment. The combination of snow coverage, ice blocks, and dramatic icicle formations makes these caves feel like natural ice caverns. The blue ice adds a striking cyan glow in places.

<Note>
The giant icicle formations can create natural columns connecting floor to ceiling, similar to dripstone caves but with an icy aesthetic.
</Note>

## Palette Configuration

The terrain generation uses:
- **Sampler Type:** Cellular with domain warp
- **Frequency:** 0.08 (surface), 0.8 (subsurface)
- **Warp Frequency:** 0.01 (surface), 0.1 (subsurface)
- **Dimensions:** 2D sampling
