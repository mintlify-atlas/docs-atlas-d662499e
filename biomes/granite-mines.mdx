Granite Mines are reddish-brown caves where granite stone is naturally abundant, creating warm-toned underground chambers.

## Overview

**Biome ID:** `GRANITE_MINES`  
**Vanilla Replacement:** `minecraft:dripstone_caves`  
**Color Code:** `#A77663`

## Terrain Palette

Granite Mines feature a simple two-material palette:

### Surface Layers (2 blocks deep)
- Stone (dominant - 3 parts)
- Granite (1 part)

### Subsurface Layer (1 block deep)
- Stone (dominant - 3 parts)
- Granite (1 part)

### Deepslate Transition
Below Y-level 7, terrain uses the `GRANITE_MINES_DEEPSLATE` palette.

<Note>
Granite appears at a 1:3 ratio with stone, creating veins and pockets throughout the cave system.
</Note>

## Features

Granite Mines are primarily defined by their terrain palette and do not include additional feature decorations.

## Environment

Granite Mines offer a straightforward mining-focused cave biome. The reddish-brown granite creates warm color accents throughout otherwise standard stone caves. These biomes feel like natural underground quarries where granite is readily accessible.

<Note>
This biome is ideal for gathering large amounts of granite for building projects without needing to search for scattered pockets.
</Note>

## Palette Configuration

The terrain generation uses:
- **Sampler Type:** Cellular with domain warp
- **Frequency:** 0.08 (surface), 0.8 (subsurface)
- **Warp Frequency:** 0.01 (surface), 0.1 (subsurface)
- **Dimensions:** 2D sampling
