Fungal Undergrowth biomes are earthy cave systems where giant mushrooms and organic materials dominate the landscape.

## Overview

**Biome ID:** `FUNGAL_UNDERGROWTH`  
**Vanilla Replacement:** `minecraft:dripstone_caves`  
**Color Code:** `#A37E77`

## Custom Colors

- **Grass Color:** `#9C734D`
- **Foliage Color:** `#9C734D`

## Terrain Palette

Fungal Undergrowth uses earthy, organic materials:

### Multi-layer Structure (4 layers, 1 block each)
- Coarse Dirt
- Rooted Dirt
- Brown Concrete Powder
- Stone (in deeper layers)

### Deepslate Transition
Below Y-level 7, terrain uses the `FUNGAL_UNDERGROWTH_DEEPSLATE` palette.

<Note>
This biome has a unique 4-layer palette structure instead of the typical 2-layer system, creating more varied surface textures.
</Note>

## Features

### Trees
- **Giant Red Mushrooms** - Massive red mushroom structures
- **Giant Brown Mushrooms** - Large brown mushroom formations

### Flora
- Small cave mushrooms
- Cave grass
- Hanging roots

## Environment

Fungal Undergrowth biomes feel like natural underground forests dominated by fungal growth. The earthy terrain combined with giant mushrooms creates a unique subterranean ecosystem. The custom grass and foliage colors give the vegetation a brownish, earthy tone that matches the overall aesthetic.

<Note>
The abundance of mushrooms makes this biome excellent for mushroom stew ingredients and mycelium-related resources.
</Note>
