The Honey Grotto is a vibrant, life-filled cave biome featuring golden honeycomb blocks, flowering plants, and massive bee nest structures.

## Overview

**Biome ID:** `HONEY_GROTTO`  
**Vanilla Replacement:** `minecraft:dripstone_caves`  
**Color Code:** `#EBCC31`

## Terrain Palette

The Honey Grotto uses a warm, golden color palette:

### Surface Layers (2 blocks deep)
- Stone
- Yellow Terracotta
- Moss Block
- Yellow Concrete

### Subsurface Layer (1 block deep)
- Stone (primary)
- Yellow Terracotta
- Honeycomb Block
- Yellow Concrete

### Deepslate Transition
Below Y-level 7, the terrain transitions to a deepslate variant using the `HONEY_GROTTO_DEEPSLATE` palette.

<Note>
The palette uses cellular noise with domain warping to create organic, varied terrain patterns.
</Note>

## Features

### Landforms
- **Giant Bee Nests** - Massive honeycomb structures that dominate the landscape

### Preprocessors
- **Lush Cave Water Pools** - Natural water pools scattered throughout

### Postprocessors
- **Honey Drips** - Decorative honey dripping effects

### Flora
- Mixed flower cover (Substratum variant)
- Moss carpet
- Cave glow berries
- Cave spore blossoms
- Small dripleaf
- Big dripleaf
- Cave grass
- Azalea bushes
- Flowering azalea bushes

## Environment

Honey Grottoes are among the most lush and vibrant cave biomes. The combination of flowering plants, moss coverage, and giant bee nests creates a unique underground ecosystem. Water pools provide additional visual interest and can serve as natural water sources.

<Note>
This biome has exceptional flora diversity, making it one of the most visually complex cave biomes.
</Note>
